# 100 Photography Effects & Terminology

## Light & Flares
1. Lens Flare — Bright spots/rays when light hits the lens directly
2. Light Leak — Random colored streaks, mimicking film defects
3. Flare — General term for parasitic reflections in optics
4. Overexposure — Too much light, loss of detail in highlights
5. Underexposure — Too little light, loss of detail in shadows
6. Glow — Soft halo around bright areas
7. Bloom — Bright light sources bleeding beyond their edges
8. Starburst — Star-shaped rays from point lights at narrow apertures
9. Halation — Reddish halo around bright objects on film
10. Sun Flare Overlay — Adding sun flare effects in post-processing

## Blur & Focus
11. Bokeh — Aesthetic background blur with soft circles of light
12. Motion Blur — Smear caused by subject or camera movement
13. Depth of Field (DOF) — Zone of acceptable sharpness in frame
14. Shallow DOF — Sharp subject with heavily blurred background
15. Soft Focus — Slight overall blur for a dreamy/romantic look
16. Tilt-Shift — Miniature effect via selective focus with lens plane shift
17. Orton Effect — Layering sharp and blurred exposures for a dreamy glow
18. Radial Blur — Blur radiating outward from a central point
19. Gaussian Blur — Smooth, even blur applied uniformly or selectively
20. Zoom Burst — Changing zoom during exposure, radial streaks from center

## Exposure & Technique
21. Long Exposure — Slow shutter to blur water, stars, lights
22. Double Exposure — Two frames superimposed on one image
23. HDR (High Dynamic Range) — Combining exposures for full tonal detail
24. Silhouette — Dark outline of subject against bright background
25. Panning — Tracking a moving subject, sharp subject with blurred background
26. Freelensing — Shooting with detached lens for unpredictable focus and leaks
27. Camera Shake — Overall image smear from handheld vibration
28. Bracketing — Taking multiple shots at different exposures for later blending
29. Bulb Mode — Manually holding the shutter open for very long exposures
30. Multiple Exposure — Combining three or more frames into a single image

## Noise & Texture
31. Grain / Film Grain — Textured noise mimicking analog film
32. Noise — Digital pixel artifacts, especially at high ISO
33. Texture Overlay — Layering textures (scratches, paper, concrete) over a photo
34. Dust & Scratches — Mimicking aged/damaged film surface
35. Rain/Snow Overlay — Adding rain or snow particles to a scene

## Optical Distortions & Aberrations
36. Chromatic Aberration — Color fringing (purple/green) along high-contrast edges
37. Barrel Distortion — Convex warping typical of wide-angle lenses
38. Pincushion Distortion — Concave warping typical of telephoto lenses
39. Fisheye — Ultra-wide 180° spherical distortion
40. Vignette — Darkening or lightening of image edges
41. Diffraction — Sharpness loss at very small apertures (f/22+)
42. Coma — Point lights rendered as comet-shaped smears at frame edges
43. Astigmatism — Lens defect turning points into lines or ellipses
44. Spherical Aberration — Soft/soapy look from imperfect lens curvature
45. Moire Pattern — Interference pattern from overlapping fine repetitive structures
46. Anamorphic Flare — Horizontal stretched flares from anamorphic lenses

## Lighting Styles
47. Chiaroscuro — Dramatic contrast between light and shadow
48. Rim Light — Backlighting that outlines the subject's edges
49. Golden Hour — Warm soft light at sunrise/sunset
50. Blue Hour — Cool bluish light just after sunset or before sunrise
51. High Key — Bright, airy tonality with minimal shadows
52. Low Key — Dark tonality with deep shadows
53. Rembrandt Lighting — Portrait light creating a triangle under one eye
54. Butterfly Lighting — Light from above creating a shadow under the nose
55. Loop Lighting — Slight shadow of the nose angled to one side

## Color & Toning
56. Saturation — Intensity/purity of color
57. Vibrance — Smart saturation boosting muted colors while protecting skin tones
58. Desaturation — Reducing color intensity
59. Color Grading — Adjusting the overall color palette of an image
60. Split Toning — Different hues applied to highlights and shadows
61. Cross-Processing — Non-standard color shifts mimicking wrong film development
62. Sepia — Warm brown tone, vintage/antique styling
63. Monochrome — Image in shades of a single color
64. Color Cast — Unwanted overall color tint (yellow, blue, etc.)
65. White Balance Shift — Intentional WB offset for creative toning
66. Teal and Orange — Popular cinematic complementary color palette
67. Bleach Bypass — Low saturation + high contrast, gritty/noir look
68. Selective Color — One color remains while the rest is black & white
69. Duotone — Two-tone image using two contrasting colors
70. Tint — Applying a uniform color wash over the image

## Sharpness & Detail
71. Contrast — Difference between the lightest and darkest areas
72. Clarity — Local mid-tone contrast enhancement
73. Sharpening — Enhancing edge and detail definition
74. Dehaze — Removing atmospheric haze to restore contrast and color
75. Unsharp Mask — Sharpening method using a blurred copy to enhance edges

## Retro & Film Effects
76. Cyanotype — Contact print process with characteristic blue tone
77. Lomo Effect — Saturated colors, heavy vignette, grain, unpredictability
78. Daguerreotype Look — Mimicking early photography: silvery tone, fine detail
79. Vintage Filter — General aged look with faded colors and warm tones
80. Faded Blacks — Lifting black levels for a matte/washed-out film look

## Composition & Perspective
81. Dutch Angle (Dutch Tilt) — Intentional camera tilt for tension and dynamism
82. Forced Perspective — Using distance/angle to make objects appear larger or smaller
83. Reflection — Using mirrors, water, or glass to create symmetrical compositions
84. Leading Lines — Lines guiding the viewer's eye through the frame
85. Frame Within a Frame — Using elements in the scene to frame the subject

## Special Effects & Stylization
86. Infrared (IR) — Shooting/simulating IR spectrum: white foliage, dark skies
87. Solarization — Partial tone inversion, psychedelic effect
88. Posterization — Reducing color gradations to flat bands
89. Glitch Effect — Digital distortions, channel shifts, simulated data corruption
90. Negative / Inversion — Reversing lights and darks (negative look)
91. Pixelation — Intentionally reducing resolution to visible blocks
92. Kaleidoscope — Mirrored repetition of image fragments
93. Prism Effect — Light refraction through a prism held before the lens
94. Lens Whacking — Tapping/shifting the lens during capture (similar to freelensing)
95. Light Painting — Drawing with a light source during long exposure
96. Steel Wool Spinning — Spinning burning steel wool for circular spark trails
97. Levitation — Composite technique making subjects appear floating
98. Multiplicity — Same person appearing multiple times in one frame via compositing
99. Miniature Effect — Making real scenes look like tiny scale models (via tilt-shift or post)
100. Frozen Motion (Freeze Frame) — Ultra-fast shutter capturing motion with zero blur
